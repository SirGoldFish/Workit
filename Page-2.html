Exercies such as jogging or walking, comes to mind as one thinks of working out. For strengthening your heart and lungs, these types of exercises are essential, but a full fitness program should also include strength exercises, stability training, and balance training. 

There are many ways to organize a strength training program, but pairing those muscle groups together is beneficial for many people. On different days, working out different body parts gives your muscles more rest between exercises and helps you stop overtraining.

  

Muscle Groups

There are wo major types of muscle group in our body: Major and Minor Muscle Group. Bodybuilding exercises are built in such a way that the main muscles (such as the chest, thighs, and back) are combined with two smaller muscle groups (such as the biceps, triceps, hamstrings, calves, stomach, and shoulders) in each exercise in a single major muscle group with two smaller muscle group workout splits.

This exercise division has two benefits: 

        1) This helps you to prioritize the large sections of the body as it is the one that gets trained in the exercise               first.

        2) It will help you get through the exercise as if in the first part of the body you are going to expend a lot of              energy, since the remaining two are smaller compared to the first one, you can finish it quickly.

What to pair together?

There is no best way to group together the muscles. Before you find one that fits best for you, you might want to play with a few different pairings. You should follow a regimen that balances all the various muscle groups if you're training for general fitness. You can benefit from stressing such muscle groups commonly used in your sport if you are training for a sport.

Schedule for workouts

The American Heart Association recommends taking at least two days between lilting sessions to give your body time to recover. Many people find they like strength training three times a week.

Here’s an example of how you could structure your weekly schedule:

Monday: arms and shoulders

Push-ups: 3 sets of 8 reps
Biceps curls: 3 sets of 8 reps
Shoulder press: 3 sets of 10 reps
Bench dips: 2 sets of 12 reps
Lateral raises: 3 sets of 10 reps


Wednesday: legs

Barbell back squats: 3 sets of 8 reps
Dumbbell lunges: 2 sets of 10 reps
Romanian deadlifts: 3 sets of 8 reps
Step-ups: 2 sets of 12 reps
Calve raises: 3 sets of 12 reps


Friday: back, chest, and abdominals


Dumbbell bench press: 3 sets of 8 reps
Dumbbell fly: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
Bicycle crunches: 3 sets of 20 reps
One-arm dumbbell rows: 3 sets of 8 reps
Dumbbell bent-over rows: 3 sets of 8 reps
Crunches: 3 sets of 20 reps
